THE BEGINNING

GitHub

GitHub was instrumental in our HRIS development training. Its robust features, including version control, issue tracking, and pull requests, significantly enhanced our collaborative efforts.

Frameworks

Our mentors strategically recommended a Tailwind CSS and Angular stack for our HRIS, emphasizing Tailwind's utility-first approach for rapid UI development and Angular's architectural strength in managing complex systems.

SCRUM

To ensure timely delivery and adaptability, we adopted a Scrum methodology, breaking down the project into manageable sprints. This agile approach, combined with the power of our tech stack, enabled us to create a robust and user-friendly HRIS.

The first week of immersing ourselves in the new frontend frameworks introduced by our mentors was akin to diving headfirst into a vast, unfamiliar ocean. The initial waves of information were overwhelming, each framework presenting its unique syntax, components, and logic. It was a steep learning curve, but with every passing day, the water began to feel less turbulent.

Our mentors, experienced surfers in this digital sea, were invaluable guides. Their patient explanations and practical demonstrations were like buoys, keeping us afloat during the initial struggles. We found ourselves spending countless hours poring over documentation, experimenting with different approaches, and seeking clarification from our mentors. Gradually, the patterns started to emerge, and the code began to make more sense.
